<h2>Chapter-1: The Etiquette of Seeker's Inner Self</h2>
<h3>The Pure Intensions in Seeking Knowledge Treated as Worship:</h3>
<ul>
<li>Seek knowledge only and only to please the God (Alone).</li>
<li>Never seek knowledge for showing off, gaining wealth, glory, fame,  	praiseworthiness etc.</li>
<li>Adhere to the loving and fearing of God openly and in secret (by staying  	away from sinful acts).</li>
<li>Seek refuge in God from devil, let your heart be filled with His love,  	and your tongue with His remembrance, and rejoice and be happy with His  	Rulings and His Wisdom. </li>
</ul>
<h3>Behaviour with Your Fellow Learners and Other Peoples</h3>
<ul>
<li>Lower your wing out of humbleness </li>
<li>Put your arrogance and pride (looking down upon people and rejecting  	truth) behind you</li>
<li>Adorn yourself with possible best manners: cheerful face, spreading  	salaam, gentle words.</li>
</ul>
<h3>You and Yourself</h3>
<ul>
<li>Follow a moderate life style of contentment.</li>
<li>Contemplate: Think before speak.</li>
<li>Enjoy firmness, confidence and patience.</li>
<li>Avoid acting foolishly, laughing loudly, continual joking, idle  	fiddling.</li>
<li>Avoid to be seen in doubtful places or with juvenile dresses.</li>
<li>Avoid gatherings of vain speech, confused movements (commotions).</li>
</ul>
